GNUTS

Groundnuts, commonly known as peanuts, are a leguminous crop widely cultivated for their edible seeds.
Despite their name, they are not true nuts but belong to the legume family, like beans and lentils.
Groundnuts grow underground, developing within pods after pollination.
They are rich in protein, healthy fats, and essential nutrients such as vitamins B and E, magnesium, and antioxidants. Widely consumed in various forms—raw,
roasted, or processed into peanut butter and oil—groundnuts play a vital role in global agriculture and nutrition. They are a staple in many diets and serve as an important source of livelihood for farmers, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions.
