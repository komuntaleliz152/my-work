MAIZE

Maize, also known as corn, is a cereal crop that originated in Mesoamerica and is now one of the most widely grown staple foods worldwide. It is a versatile plant used for human consumption, livestock feed, and industrial products such as biofuels and biodegradable plastics. Maize thrives in a variety of climates and soil types, making it a crucial crop for global food security. Rich in carbohydrates and essential nutrients, it serves as a dietary staple in many cultures, especially in the Americas, Africa, and Asia. Beyond its agricultural importance, maize also holds cultural and historical significance, particularly among indigenous communities.
